Australian Antarctic Territory

Who owns Antarctica?

Antarctica is a largely forgotten continent located at the south pole of the planet, and while no one can “own” Antarctica due to an international treaty, some countries including Australia have claimed territories there. The map below shows the area claimed by each county (note how Argentina, Chile and the UK's Antarctic claims are overlapping).

Bases of Antarctic operations

Australia is among many countries to have multiple Antarctic bases across the continent, these bases are mostly used for scientific research and can range from small camps to large basses equipped to support over one hundred people year round.

RSV Nuyina: The key to Australian Antarctic operations

Transport and research in the Antarctic is largely conduced with large icebreaker ships, in Australia we only have one large ship used for Antarctic voyages, the RSV Nuyina. The RSV Nuyina is a capable of carrying up to 32 crew and 117 passengers, it is compared to the other 33 Antarctic vessels below.
